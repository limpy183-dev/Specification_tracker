Global Migration
1. What are the contemporary patterns of global migration?
Dynamic Flows: Current spatial patterns, numbers, and the composition/direction of international migrant flows (inter-regional and intra-regional).
Migration and Development: The relationship between migration and socio-economic development using indices like Human Development Index (HDI) and the value of migrant remittances.
Global Impacts: How migration promotes stability, growth, and development versus how it causes inequalities, conflicts, and injustices.
2. Why has migration become increasingly complex?
Complexity in the 21st Century: The influence of economic globalisation, the emergence of new source/host areas, and the high concentration of young and female migrants.
Global Shifts: The rise of South-South corridors (now equal to South-North) and the increase in refugees due to conflict and persecution.
Policy and Corridors: Changes in national immigration/emigration policies and the development of distinct bi-lateral flow corridors.
EDC Case Study: A case study of one Emerging and Developing Country (EDC), exploring its immigration/emigration patterns, interdependence with other countries, and the resulting impacts on stability and equality.
3. What are the issues associated with unequal flows of global migration?
Power Relations: How unequal power between countries creates specific opportunities and challenges.
AC Case Study: A case study of one Advanced Country (AC) to show how it drives the migration system, focusing on labour supply opportunities and border issues.
LIDC Case Study: A case study of one Low-Income Developing Country (LIDC) to show its limited influence on the global system, focusing on the benefits of remittances versus the "brain drain" loss of skilled workers.
